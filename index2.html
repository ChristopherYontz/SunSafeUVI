What is the UV Index ?

The UV Index is a tool you can use to protect yourself from UV radiation. It tells you the times during the day that you need to be SunSmart.

The UV Idex divides UV radiation levels into:

  • low (1-2)
  • moderate (3-5)
  • high (6-7)
  • very high (8-10)
  • extreme (11 and above)

What can I do to protect myself from the sun?

Sunscreen

Use broad spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30. Put a thick layer on all exposed skin. Reapply at least every 2 hours. Reapply sooner after swimming, sweating, or towel drying.

Protective Clothing

Wear long-sleeved shirts, long pants and skirts, sunglasses, and wide-brimmed hats. Clothes made from tightly woven fabric offer the best protection. Clothing certified to offer UV protection is best.

Shade

Avoid being in the sun at peak hours. Stay in the shade under an umbrella, tree, or other shelter. Your best bet is to use sunscreen and/or wear protective clothing even in the shade.

Why you should use sun protection

Exhibit A: Truck Driver Face

According to CBS News, an unnamed truck driver spent 28 years on the road. Due to prolonged exposure to the sun on the left side of his face, it experienced significantly more damage than the right side. This chronic exposure to UVA rays resulted in the development of unilateral dermatoheliosis, a condition characterized by gradual skin thickening and the breakdown of elastic fibers.

Sun damage can cause premature aging, skin cancer, skin discoloration, and eye damage, so it is important to take proper percautions.
